About

With a background in academic research (philosophy and cognitive science), I’m a lifelong learner. I bring the experience of systematic thinking across a variety of problem domains, and the ability to communicate complex concepts to my development work.

Having focused on cognitive bias, as well as research communication, in previous roles, I am passionated about building applications which make abstract information easy to understand and which are enjoyable to use.

Whether it’s TDD and thinking about which edge cases, or how to best represent the relationships between objects I’m modelling, I really enjoy the process of solving problems with code, talking about my process, and learning from others I’m working with.

I like spending time in nature, hiking, cycling and rock-climbing (sport and easy trad). Music is also a big part of my life. I’m currently playing accordion, cello and loop pedal (Boss RC300), and am one third of the Bristol folk band Other Tongues (that’s us above), along with Michal Nahman and Jenny Sansom.
